Course Projects and Weekly Work

Over the course of DS 4200, I had the opportunity to explore a wide variety of visualization and design concepts, ranging from data cleaning and preprocessing to creating fully interactive dashboards. Each week brought a new challenge— from learning D3.js and understanding its data-binding mechanism to exploring advanced layouts such as choropleth maps and network visualizations.

These projects allowed me to experiment with color theory, typography, and storytelling to convey insights effectively. Beyond the technical aspects, I also learned how design choices influence perception and interpretation of data. Working collaboratively through GitHub, I gained experience managing repositories, handling pull requests, and maintaining code consistency throughout team projects.

Below, I’ll continue to add updates to my weekly progress and visualizations that showcase the learning process and creativity involved in building these data-driven stories.
